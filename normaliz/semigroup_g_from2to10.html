Numerical Semigroups — genus 2 to 10

Each polytope is the Waldi Polytope cut by fixing multiplicity m, first Apéry element w1 = mt+1, and Selmer sum ∑wi = mg+m(m−1)/2. The Waldi Polytope is an affine transformation of the standard Kunz Polytope from the literature (the latter uses qi = (wi−i)/m). Each lattice point is one numerical semigroup. The columns ci,1 are the entries of the first column of the reduced Kunz matrix Cred (the ambient variable Normaliz reports); the remaining columns mirror the in-app shortprops view (f, e, σ, r, ra, fg, t, Sym, generators, PF, Wilf, 1/e).
