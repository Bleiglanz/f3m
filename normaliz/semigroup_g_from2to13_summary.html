genus = number of gaps.
N(g)
total number of numerical semigroups of genus g.
N'(g) c1,1=0
those whose reduced Kunz coefficient c1,1=0, equivalently w1=m+1.
N''(g) w1∈gen
those where the smallest non-zero Apéry element w1 is a minimal generator.
Nsym(g)
symmetric semigroups: type t=1, equivalently g=(f+1)/2.
Nasym(g)
almost-symmetric semigroups: f+t=2g, equivalently ra=r and PF(S)∖{f} equals the set of reflected gaps. Includes the symmetric case (t=1, f+1=2g).
f<m, m<f<2m, 2m<f<3m, 3m<f
partition of all semigroups by where the Frobenius number sits relative to the multiplicity. f never equals km for k ≥ 1 since km ∈ S.
ae=f+m=2g+1, ae=f+m=2g
counts of semigroups in which f+m is itself the largest minimal generator (ae=f+m) and additionally equals 2g+1 or 2g, respectively. Empirically these come paired with r=m−2 and r=m−1 (asserted at runtime).
∃ri=2
semigroups where the count of reflected gaps in some residue class i ∈ 1..m equals 2 — a coarse predicate for the closure step S ↦ S ∪ {f}.
shift←(g)
conjectural source of the S ↦ S ∪ {f} shift: S of genus g that is almost-symmetric, has every Apéry element > 2m, and level ≥ 3 (i.e. f − m > 2m). Empirically S′ = S ∪ {f} then lies in shift→(g−1).
shift→(g)
conjectural target of the shift: S of genus g with f+m a minimal generator, every Apéry element > 2m, and every ri ∈ {1, 2} for i ≠ μ. Conjectured: shift←(g+1) = shift→(g).

Empirical: f<m is always 1 (the unique ordinary semigroup ⟨g+1,…,2g+1⟩); m<f<2m matches OEIS A000071 (F(n+2)−1) for g = 2..10: 1, 2, 4, 7, 12, 20, 33, 54, 88.

The ae=f+m=2g column is not in bijection with Nasym(g+1) via the closure map S ↦ S ∪ {f}. Reason: for every g ≥ 2 there is exactly one almost-symmetric S of genus g+1 whose closure is the ordinary semigroup of genus g, and ordinary always satisfies ae=f+m=2g+1 — so it lands in the neighbouring ae=f+m=2g+1 column instead. (For g+1=2 that S is ⟨3,4,5⟩ itself; for g+1 ≥ 3 it is ⟨g+1, g+2, …, 2g+1, 2g+3⟩.) For g+1 = 2..10 this accounts for 9 of the 30 almost-symmetric semigroups; the remaining 21 do close into the ae=f+m=2g column at genus g.
