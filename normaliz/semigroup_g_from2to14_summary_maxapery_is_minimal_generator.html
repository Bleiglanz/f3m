Numerical Semigroups — genus 2 to 14 (summary, filter: maxapery_is_minimal_generator)

Aggregate counts restricted to: Numerical semigroups in which f+m is itself a minimal generator (equivalently, the largest Apéry value wm−1 = f+m and ae = f+m). Empirically these are the only candidates for which closing the last gap S ↦ S ∪ {f} lands inside the well-behaved Kunz-cone neighbour.

For the full unfiltered summary see semigroup_g_from2to14_summary.html.

counts of semigroups in which f+m is itself the largest minimal generator (ae=f+m) and additionally equals 2g+1 or 2g, respectively. Empirically these come paired with r=m−2 and r=m−1 (asserted at runtime).

Empirical: f<m is always 1 (the unique ordinary semigroup ⟨g+1,…,2g+1⟩); m<f<2m matches OEIS A000071 (F(n+2)−1) for g = 2..10: 1, 2, 4, 7, 12, 20, 33, 54, 88.

The ae=f+m=2g column is not in bijection with Nasym(g+1) via the closure map S ↦ S ∪ {f}. Reason: for every g ≥ 2 there is exactly one almost-symmetric S of genus g+1 whose closure is the ordinary semigroup of genus g, and ordinary always satisfies ae=f+m=2g+1 — so it lands in the neighbouring ae=f+m=2g+1 column instead. (For g+1=2 that S is ⟨3,4,5⟩ itself; for g+1 ≥ 3 it is ⟨g+1, g+2, …, 2g+1, 2g+3⟩.) For g+1 = 2..10 this accounts for 9 of the 30 almost-symmetric semigroups; the remaining 21 do close into the ae=f+m=2g column at genus g.
